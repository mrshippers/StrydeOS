# How NPS Works in StrydeOS

Zero setup. Zero effort. Patients get asked, you get the score.

---

## What happens

When a patient finishes their treatment, StrydeOS sends them a single text message:

> "Hi Sarah, thank you for choosing Spires Physiotherapy. On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend us? Simply reply with a number."

That's it. The patient taps a number. Everything else is automatic.

---

## The timeline

| Day | What happens |
|-----|-------------|
| **Day 0** | Patient's appointment is completed in your PMS |
| **Day 3** | Patient receives the NPS text |
| **Within minutes** | Patient replies with a number (e.g. "9") |
| **Day 5** | If they scored 9 or 10, they get a follow-up asking for a Google Review |

No app downloads. No portals. No links to click. Just reply to a text.

---

## How patients are categorised

| Score | Category | What it means |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **9-10** | Promoter | Loves you. Will recommend you. Gets a Google Review nudge. |
| **7-8** | Passive | Fine but not enthused. No action needed. |
| **0-6** | Detractor | Something went wrong. Needs follow-up fast. |

The NPS formula: **% Promoters minus % Detractors = your NPS score** (-100 to +100).

A score above 50 is excellent. Above 70 is world-class.

---

## What the clinic owner sees

### Dashboard (Intelligence > Reputation tab)

- **NPS score** with monthly trend line
- **Breakdown** of promoters, passives, and detractors
- **Google Reviews** count and monthly velocity
- **Per-clinician scores** so you know who's generating promoters and who isn't

### Alerts

When a patient scores 0-6 (detractor), the clinic owner gets an **immediate email alert** with:

- The score
- Which clinician treated that patient
- A suggested action: "Reach out within 24 hours"

This gives you a window to recover the relationship before a bad Google Review lands.

---

## The Google Review pipeline

This is where NPS directly drives revenue:

1. Patient scores 9 or 10 (promoter)
2. Two days later, they get a text: *"If you have a moment, a Google Review would mean the world to us"* with your Google Maps link
3. Happy patients leave reviews while the experience is still fresh

More 5-star reviews = higher Google ranking = more new patients finding you.

---

## What the clinic needs to provide

**One thing:** your Google Business Profile URL (the Google Maps link for your clinic).

That's it. Everything else is automatic from the moment your PMS is connected.

If you don't have the Google link yet, NPS collection still runs — you just won't get the promoter-to-review nudge until you add it.

---

## Safeguards

- **Cooldown:** The same patient won't be surveyed more than once every 30 days
- **Opt-out:** If a patient replies "STOP", they're removed from all future messages
- **Privacy:** NPS data is stored per-clinic, never shared between clinics
- **No spam:** Only one text (two if they're a promoter). That's the entire sequence.

---

## Why this matters for your clinic

Most private practices have no idea what their patients actually think. You find out when a bad Google Review appears — by which time it's too late.

NPS closes that gap:

- **Detractors** get caught early, before they go public
- **Promoters** get channelled into Google Reviews, where they drive new patient acquisition
- **Per-clinician scores** show you who needs coaching and who deserves recognition
- **Trends over time** show whether changes you're making are actually improving patient experience

You can't manage what you can't measure. NPS makes patient satisfaction measurable — automatically, from day one.
