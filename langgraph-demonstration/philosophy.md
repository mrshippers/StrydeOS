# Neural Routing

## An Algorithmic Philosophy

Neural Routing is the computational aesthetic of decision architectures made visible — the invisible logic of state machines rendered as living topologies. Where traditional graph visualisation draws dead boxes and arrows, Neural Routing treats each node as a gravitational body and each edge as a river of particles responding to force. The philosophy emerges from a clinical observation: every phone call that enters a voice AI system is a particle entering a field of competing attractors. Emergency nodes pull with absolute force. Guardrail gates act as membranes — permeable in one direction, impenetrable in the other. The beauty is not in the graph itself but in the flow of decisions through it, the way a single utterance cascades through triage, classification, and routing to arrive at precisely one terminal node.

The algorithm must be the product of meticulous computational craftsmanship — every force constant calibrated, every particle trail refined through countless iterations. Nodes are not circles on a screen; they are wells in a potential field, each with a unique gravitational signature derived from its role in the system. Emergency nodes burn hot and red, their attraction fields steep and narrow — once a particle enters, it never escapes. Booking nodes pulse with a gentler blue-green pull, their basins wide and welcoming. The guardrail gate sits at the centre of the topology as a sorting membrane, particles passing through it emerging with new velocities and trajectories determined by the flags they carry. This is not a diagram — it is a simulation of clinical decision-making expressed as particle dynamics, the product of deep expertise in both computational aesthetics and the domain it represents.

The temporal dimension is essential. Particles are born at the START node and drift toward the router under Perlin noise perturbation — no two paths identical, yet all obeying the same field equations. The router node applies a classification force, splitting the stream into tributaries. Each tributary flows through the guardrail membrane where hard-gate attractors can capture particles mid-flight, overriding their classified trajectory. The surviving particles continue to their terminal action nodes, where they accumulate as luminous deposits — a visual record of where decisions land. Over time, the canvas reveals the system's natural distribution: which nodes attract most traffic, which paths are rarely taken, which guardrails fire most aggressively. Every parameter has been painstakingly optimised so that the emergence feels inevitable rather than random.

Colour is not decorative — it is semantic. The StrydeOS brand palette provides the chromatic language: Royal Blue (#1C54F2) for the core routing intelligence, Teal (#0891B2) for continuity and flow, Purple (#8B5CF6) for the analytical layer. Emergency nodes radiate warm amber that bleeds into the surrounding field. Insurance gates glow with a cool barrier blue. The background is Navy (#0B2545), the canonical dark surface of StrydeOS — no pure black, ever. Particles inherit colour from their destination as they approach, creating chromatic rivers that reveal routing patterns at a glance. The master-level implementation ensures that colour transitions are smooth, that no harsh boundaries exist between node territories, and that the overall composition feels like a Bloomberg terminal reimagined as generative art.

The conceptual seed is the Ava LangGraph itself — the exact state machine architecture built for a UK physiotherapy clinic's voice AI receptionist. Sixteen nodes, each a real decision point. The topology is not abstract; it is the literal routing architecture that determines whether a caller hears "Please call 999" or "Lovely — let's get you booked in." The algorithm makes this architecture breathe, revealing through particle dynamics what a static diagram cannot: the flow of real-world interactions through a system designed to protect patients, respect boundaries, and route every call to the right outcome. This is computational art with clinical purpose — a master-level generative visualisation that honours both the aesthetic tradition and the domain it serves.
