The Heidi question.
Smaller gap than the funding suggests.

A surface read on Heidi Health's actual architecture, the AutoMedica acquisition, and whether the lead AI healthcare company is genuinely in StrydeOS's operational lane. Spoiler: they're not. But the layer brushes against Ava.

Three things in 30 seconds
  • Heidi is a frontier-model wrapper, not a model company. Multi-model on AWS Bedrock plus self-hosted EKS, MongoDB Atlas Vector Search, Claude for Evidence. The moat is distribution, brand, regulatory paperwork, eval harness — not irreplicable AI. Funding ≠ engineering lead.
  • AutoMedica was a £0-VC two-surgeon UK regulatory acquihire. Heidi bought MHRA AI Airlock credentials and NHS-facing relationships, not a substantial tech team. Signals serious UK NHS-tier intent, not private physio ops intent.
  • Heidi Comms brushes Ava on the surface, doesn't touch Pulse or Intelligence. No per-clinician operational KPIs, English-only, sales-only, no TM3 or WriteUpp integration. Heidi sits in the same clinic as StrydeOS, underneath the operational layer.

01 — Architecture Truth

What's actually under Heidi's bonnet

Sourced from AWS re:Invent 2025 talk by senior engineer I Gede Cakramurti (via ZenML LLMOps DB), MongoDB case study, PromptHub success story, Kinde customer story, Anthropic's Claude for Healthcare announcement, and current job postings.

No "Heidi model" — multi-model frontier wrapper

Hybrid Amazon Bedrock for immediate availability plus self-hosted models on EKS for control. Heidi Evidence is "in part built on Claude" per Heidi's 24 Feb 2026 announcement; Heidi is named on Anthropic's Claude for Healthcare partner list alongside Banner Health, Novo Nordisk, Elation Health, Viz.ai. Their Medical AI Resident told PromptHub they use it because "models are added to the platform as soon as they are released." Translation: model-agnostic, swap frontier models per task and region.

No engineering content — re:Invent talk, MongoDB writeup, careers JDs, Anthropic case study — mentions Heidi pre-training a foundation model. JDs describe fine-tuning, RAG, eval work. Not foundation R&D.

honest verdict on the moat

A competent team could build a 70–80% equivalent scribe in 3–6 engineering months with Claude + clinical templates + a decent eval harness. The remaining 20–30% — edge-case clinical accuracy, template depth, EHR integrations, regulatory paperwork — is what burns 18 months and what the $96.6M pays for. Abridge, Nabla, Freed, Suki, Nuance DAX, Ambience, DeepScribe all use roughly the same pattern. The moat is brand, distribution, and integration depth, not model quality.

05 — Recommendations

Six moves

Posture: treat Heidi as the dominant scribe-and-clinician-AI layer that sits underneath StrydeOS in the same clinic. Never get pulled into a note-generation arms race.

01
Stop framing Heidi as a competitor on positioning slides
Frame them as a parallel product the clinic already uses. New pitch: "we work above the layer Heidi serves — we don't write your notes, we tell your owner who's leaking patients and why."
02
Make TM3 + WriteUpp integration depth a near-term moat
Heidi Comms doesn't integrate with either, and they are the dominant UK private physio PMSes. Bidirectional sync depth here, before Heidi gets there, is genuine defensibility.
03
Own the "clinic-owner dashboard" identity hard
This is where Heidi structurally won't go in the next 18 months — their entire pricing and GTM is per-clinician. The per-clinician KPI angle in Intelligence is the wedge Heidi cannot replicate without breaking its own business model.
04
Assume Comms reaches Ava feature parity on voice + booking within 12 months
Edge is not voice quality — it's PMS integration depth, lifecycle retention logic, and the fact Ava plugs into Intelligence. Sell the bundle, not the receptionist.
05
Read AutoMedica correctly
This is Heidi getting NHS-side regulatory credibility, not entering UK private physio operational software. Two different markets, two different buyers. Don't let the deal pull StrydeOS into framing them as a UK ops competitor.
06
No note-generation. Ever.
Notes are downstream of someone else's job. Clinics already pay Heidi or WriteUpp for that. Building it duplicates spend, blurs the operational positioning, and pulls engineering off the moat work.
06 — Watch Signals

Two events that flip the read

If either happens, immediate strategy review. Neither has happened.

Signal A — Heidi launches a per-clinic admin pricing tier with operational analytics. Their per-clinician pricing model (rebranded Clinician plan: $150/month annual, up from $90/month) reinforces today's clinician-first GTM. Flipping to per-clinic owner-side analytics would mean breaking that model.
Signal B — Heidi acquires a UK practice-management or physio-specific operational software company. Either would mean they're pivoting toward the practice owner — direct conflict with their own PMS integration partners (Cliniko, TM3, WriteUpp, BP, MediRecords), which they've publicly disavowed via partnership-not-replacement positioning.

Closing read · what this means for stryde

Heidi is a richer, larger, faster-moving company than StrydeOS, with better distribution and bigger model spend — and they are not in our lane. They are in the clinician's pocket; we are on the clinic owner's dashboard. Comms will brush Ava on calls and booking, but ships as a per-clinician add-on with no operational analytics, English-only, sales-only, currently no TM3 or WriteUpp. None of that is true of Ava in UK private physio. AutoMedica tells us Heidi is serious about the UK — but the NHS-tier regulator route, not private physio operations. The right posture is to treat Heidi as the dominant scribe layer that sits underneath us in the same clinic, double down on Ava + Intelligence + Pulse as the operational layer that depends on PMSes Heidi doesn't reach, and never fight a note-generation war we neither want nor need.
