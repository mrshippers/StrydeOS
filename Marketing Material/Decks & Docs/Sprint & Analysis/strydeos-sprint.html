pricing deck · honest cuts six observations on canon
01

Clinic tier scales the wrong way.

£399 for 6 plus clinicians works out at £66 each. At 10 it's £40. At 15 it's £26. Pricing power leaks exactly where the customer can pay most. Cap clinician count per tier, or move to base fee plus per seat.

leaks revenue
02

Solo cannibalises Studio.

A two-clinician partnership running lean buys Solo and shares the login. You won't catch it until churn data lands. Fix at the auth layer (single seat lock) or strip Ava from Solo (call routing isn't a one-line workflow anyway).

structural risk
03

Hero anchors on cost-cutting.

"Less than the receptionist" frames StrydeOS as a cheaper hire. Defensive. The buyer most likely to pay £299 is thinking growth, not headcount. Lead on revenue captured. Receptionist comparison belongs on slide 3.

positioning
04

Path-to-£1M wants 280 customers.

UK private MSK ICP is roughly 5,000 to 8,000 practices. 4 to 6 percent share. Achievable but tight. Pricing power on the Clinic tier is the lever that buys breathing room, not customer count.

market math
05

Annual prepay buried.

Two months free is a slide-5 footnote. That's £59,800 in January with zero billing risk. Should be a tier toggle, not a postscript. Cash compression is too useful to bury.

easy fix
06

Setup fee floats unannounced.

£3,980 setup (one-time) appears on slide 5 in the maths but never on the tier cards or in the receptionist comparison. A clinic owner reading top-down sees Studio at £299 and then a £3,980 number they didn't expect. Surface it on slide 1, or fold it into onboarding.
