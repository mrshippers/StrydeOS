The cashflow lever

Twelve months upfront.
Two months free.

The standard SaaS prepay play. A 16.7% effective discount in exchange for the year collected in January. The trade looks like revenue lost on the income statement and like runway gained on the bank statement. The bank statement wins for a bootstrapped founder.

Monthly billing · 20 clinics
£5,980/mo
£71,760 collected over 12 months
Cash arrives in twelve equal slices. Churn risk lives across the whole year. If a clinic cancels in March, you keep 3 months of revenue and lose 9.
Annual prepay · 20 clinics
£59,800in Jan
12 months locked · £2,990 per customer
A full year of revenue cleared on day one, plus £3,980 setup cash. £63,780 in the bank before the first February invoice. Zero billing risk for 12 months.
The trade is £11,960 of nominal revenue for 12 months of certainty. For a bootstrapped founder running multiple ventures, the prepay path is almost always the right call. It eliminates first-year churn risk on contract, smooths out cashflow lumpiness, and converts 'will this customer pay next month' into a settled question.
