Positioning · StrydeOS vs Heidi

Different buyer.
Different problem.
Different price point.

Two products that appear in the same sentence sometimes. They shouldn't.

01 — The buyer
Heidi built a platform for hospitals and NHS GP practices. We built an operating system for private physio clinic owners.

Heidi's buyer sits inside an NHS trust or a GP surgery. They're navigating EMIS compatibility, CQC compliance, and procurement approval from a clinical informatics lead. The product serves their needs — and it does it well. Our buyer runs a 2-to-8 clinician private practice and thinks like a business operator. They care about rebooking rates, chair fill, revenue per session, and whether their Google rating is going to cost them referrals. Different org, different cycle, different definition of success. There's no overlap in who's writing the cheque.

02 — The problem
Heidi solves a documentation problem. We solve an operations problem.

Heidi gets clinical notes written faster. That's valuable — which is why StrydeOS integrates with it and doesn't try to replace it. StrydeOS answers a different set of questions: why is one of your clinicians at a 2.4 follow-up rate while another is at 4.2, and what's the revenue cost of that gap? Why did 30% of new patient enquiries go unanswered last month? Which patients stopped rebooking after session two, and did anyone notice before they walked to a competitor? Those aren't documentation questions. They're operations questions. The answer isn't a better note — it's a system that sees the whole picture: front desk, retention, and clinical performance in one place.

03 — The price point
Heidi charges per seat for documentation. StrydeOS is £299/month for the whole operating layer.

Per-seat documentation pricing makes sense when the product is a clinical tool. It doesn't map to what StrydeOS does. The Full Stack — front-desk intelligence, patient retention, and performance analytics — is £299/month for a studio-size practice. Not per clinician. Not per appointment. For the practice. The comparison doesn't hold once you understand both products clearly — which is exactly why it's worth stating directly. They're not the same budget line, they're not solving the same problem, and running both is the right answer for a practice that wants to document well and operate well. Most practices only think about one of those. StrydeOS is for the ones who've started thinking about both.
