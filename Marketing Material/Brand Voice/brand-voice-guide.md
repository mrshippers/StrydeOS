# StrydeOS — Brand Voice Guide

> The global reference. When you're not sure what StrydeOS or Driiva should sound like, start here.

---

## The ideology (load-bearing)

Every independent clinic owner should have the tools to build a million-pound practice — if they want. They're being gatekept, not by talent gaps, but by infrastructure. PE-backed chains built their national networks on data, systems, and workflow optimisation. Independent practices deserve the same. StrydeOS hands that infrastructure to the three-room practice in Sheffield, Bristol, Birmingham, or Clapham. That's the point. PPB research confirmed the opportunity. The tools existed — they were just priced for a 40-site network.

StrydeOS is not about replacing people or promoting tech over workers. It's about efficiency, consistency, ease of use, clear revenue visibility, and optimised workflows. The framing is always: the infrastructure to compete. The tools to build what you want.

## The bar

> "Would I send this to Joe without editing?"

Not "is this premium" or "is this direct." That specific question. Joe is a business partner with deep combined clinical and commercial knowledge. He'd clock a template in two sentences.

---

## Voice in one line

Quartr precision meets cultural fluency. Data-first, then the punchline. Earned confidence, not performed confidence.

---

## The ICP

Private physio practice owner. Typically 30s–50s. Running 2–10 clinicians. Smart, time-poor, business-minded. Frustrated by admin, not by ambition. Probably has a strong sense of what their clinic should be and feels like the infrastructure never quite catches up. Not a tech sceptic — just hasn't been given a reason to trust it yet. Responds to specificity, ROI, and being spoken to as a peer. Immediately clocks anything that sounds assembled.

---

## Structural rules

- Lead with the specific number, follow with the implication. Never the reverse.
- One idea per headline. Don't stack two payoffs.
- Trailing implications beat spelling it out. "£128K between them. That's not talent." — the reader fills in "it's data." Let them.
- Not every line is a hard stop. Mix questions, incomplete thoughts, mid-beat pauses.
- Rule of three is AI. Two is sharper.
- No "What if..." setups. State the fact.
- Avoid back-to-back identical patterns (three stat → implication lines in a row reads assembled, not written).
- UK geography — Sheffield, Bristol, Manchester, Birmingham, Edinburgh, Clapham. Real cities, real places.

---

## What it never sounds like

No: revolutionising, game-changing, seamlessly, leveraging, harnessing the power of, at the forefront of, in today's landscape, journey, unlock, empower, showcase, highlight, underscore, tapestry, pivotal, vibrant, groundbreaking, nestled, testament, crucial, delve, foster, enhance, align with.

No: "You don't have to be one of them." / "The future looks bright." / "Zero guesswork." / "Works weekends." / "No sick days. No notice period."

No punching at workers. No anti-staff framing. Ever.

## Module tone notes

**Intelligence** — clinical precision, no fluff. Numbers earn their place. Every stat connects to revenue or outcomes.

**Ava** — collaborative, not replacement. She handles the volume; the team does the work that needs them. Ava has a personality. Use it. (See Ava Personality Brief.)

**Pulse** — patient outcomes framing. Between sessions is where relationships either hold or disappear. Pulse lives there.
